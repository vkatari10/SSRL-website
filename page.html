Low-Earth Acessible Research Network Satellite (LEARNSat)

Developed By the UGA SSRL

A new mission giving high-school students opportunities to work with and break into Cube Sat technology. By making the prorcess easier to understand and complete, this offers a perfect way for high schoolers to break into STEM related activities.

- Lowers barriers to entry for CubeSat technology

- Lower costs

- Lower technical requirements

Competition & Targets

Developed satellite buses and kits by the SSRL are given to high schools in the form of educational LEARNsat kits and the satellite bus itself.

High schoolers are able to complete assembly of the bus using these instructions and educational kits

Launched using weather balloons, this mission operates as a suborbital mission rather than going to space, making the entire process easier to complete.

With this simplified process, this makes the launch cheaper and less technically challenging
